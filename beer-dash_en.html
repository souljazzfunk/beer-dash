Africa

Where the continent's oldest fermentation traditions coexist with the world's fastest-growing commercial beer market (6.7% annually). South Africa leads production with 37 million hectoliters, maintaining Umqombothi traditions central to Xhosa culture while supporting global operations through former SABMiller (now AB InBev) networks. Sorghum beer production is known as "the traditional beer of Africa," called regionally as chibuku, pito, dolo, burukutu, and various local names, employing sophisticated 7-12 day processes (malting, lactic fermentation, natural yeast cultivation) to produce nutritious beverages classified by FAO as "more food than drink." Ethiopian Tej honey wine uses 80% of the country's 45,300 tons annual honey production, bittered with gesho (Rhamnus prinoides).

Oceania

Achieving world-leading hop breeding excellence and cultural integration. Australia produces revolutionary hop varieties including Galaxy (1,007 tons), Vic Secret, and Enigma that define modern global brewing while maintaining beer-barbecue cultural traditions and supporting 430+ independent breweries with 20.3% craft market share. New Zealand achieves remarkable craft beer density with 200 breweries serving 5 million people, producing world-renowned hop varieties like Nelson Sauvin, Riwaka, and Motueka. The distinctive "New Zealand Draught" style represents the nation's unique amber ale-lager hybrid, while craft breweries like Garage Project achieve international recognition holding 9/10 top-rated NZ beers globally. Pacific Island cultures maintain traditional kava (Piper methysticum) ceremonies alongside imported beer preferences, with archaeological evidence showing 3,000+ years of kava cultivation across Polynesia, Melanesia, and Micronesia for ceremonial and social functions.
